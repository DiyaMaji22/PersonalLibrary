About Agatha Christie

Dame Agatha Mary Clarissa Christie, Lady Mallowan, was an English writer known for her 66 detective novels and 14 short story collections.

Christie was born into a wealthy upper-middle-class family in Torquay, Devon. She served in a hospital during the First World War before settling into writing.

Guinness World Records lists Christie as the best-selling fiction writer of all time, her novels having sold more than two billion copies.
